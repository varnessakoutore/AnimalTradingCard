Royal Peacock

Peacocks are well known for their bright ornamental wings sprouting, but not all of them are colorful. Meet the graceful, white, Royal Peackcock

  • Fact 1: White peacocks have a genetic mutation called leucism, which is why their feathers are white.
  • Fact 2: They are specially bred to be white and actually born yellow.
  • Fact 3: As they mature, their fathers turn white which is arounf 1-3 years, same as normal peacocks.
  • Fun Fact: They can end up partially white which gives their feathers a beautiful unique design.

In order for peacocks to be white, they have to receive the gene from both parents. You can find more of them in captivity today because they are not only specially bred, but also sold for around $175-$250 a piece. Not everyone exploit them though, in Buddhism, the white peacocks symbolize nirvana and in Hinduism they are believed to bring luck and prosperity.
